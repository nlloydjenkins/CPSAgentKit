## POS-PURPOSE — Purpose and stakeholder model

- **POS-PURPOSE-1.** *Helping Britain Prosper* is the anchor of strategy; purpose is positioned as integral to value creation and not separate from financial performance. *(Standing position, 2021–present.)*
- **POS-PURPOSE-2.** Argues a "healthy business in a thriving economy" framing: as Lloyds becomes more profitable, so do those around it. *(Standing position, 2021–present.)*
- **POS-PURPOSE-3.** Strategy is built so that "broader stakeholder outcomes" are embedded into "each part of how we create value", not added on top. *(2022 strategy refresh.)*

## POS-GROWTH — Growth and diversification

- **POS-GROWTH-1.** Lloyds' strategy under his leadership is explicitly oriented toward *growth*, with approximately two-thirds of the ~£3bn 2022–24 strategic investment "aligned to growing and diversifying revenue". *(2022 strategy refresh, investment envelope 2022–2024.)*
- **POS-GROWTH-2.** Four publicly stated growth opportunities for Lloyds: (1) deepen consumer relationships and cross-sell; (2) launch a *mass affluent* proposition across banking, protection and investments; (3) digitise and diversify SME banking; (4) disciplined growth of corporate and institutional banking in areas of existing strength. *(2022 strategy refresh.)*
- **POS-GROWTH-3.** Cross-sell logic: Lloyds currently provides ~2.4 of the 7 financial products an average UK consumer holds; every 5% increase in needs met is cited as a potential ~£200m additional annual revenue. *(As cited 2022.)*
- **POS-GROWTH-4.** Growth must be "disciplined" — paired with maintained cost and capital discipline; not growth at any price. *(Standing position, 2022 strategy refresh.)*

## POS-DIGITAL — Digital, data, and technology

- **POS-DIGITAL-1.** Modernising the technology estate is a strategic priority — explicit aim to be "faster than we have been historically" and to use data and end-to-end efficiency more effectively. *(Standing position, 2022 strategy refresh.)*
- **POS-DIGITAL-2.** Investing in technology is framed as necessary to avoid both *cost inflation exceeding revenue* and *losing market share* — the cost of not investing is the larger risk. *(Standing position, 2022 strategy refresh.)*
- **POS-DIGITAL-3.** Publicly cited targets: ~15% reduction in legacy applications, ~20% of applications on cloud, ~60% automation of new lending decisions by 2024. *(2022 strategy refresh, targeted by 2024.)*
- **POS-DIGITAL-4.** Acknowledges legacy IT risk (with explicit reference to TSB's 2018 IT failure as a cautionary lesson) — supports modernisation at pace, but avoids "dramatic changes" that destabilise the customer. *(Standing position, 2022.)*
- **POS-DIGITAL-5.** Open to fintech M&A *only* where it "improves the core business" — not acquisition for its own sake. *(Standing position, 2022.)*
- **POS-DIGITAL-6.** Lloyds is positioned as the UK's largest digital bank — ~18.3m digitally active users, "greater than all the neo-banks combined" — used to justify continued investment. *(As cited 2022.)*

## POS-CHANNELS — Branches, channels, and customer experience

- **POS-CHANNELS-1.** Defends a *multi-channel* (digital + branch + relationship) model. A digital-only proposition is publicly described as unable to deliver the "proper revenue per customer to build sustainable shareholder return". *(Standing position, 2022.)*
- **POS-CHANNELS-2.** Trusted multi-channel relationships are presented as "the only proven way" to build deep, profitable customer relationships long-term. *(Standing position, 2022.)*
- **POS-CHANNELS-3.** Short-term cost-to-serve pressure is acknowledged ("might be uncomfortable from a three-year perspective") but justified by long-term shifts to digital improving efficiency over time. *(Standing position, 2022.)*

## POS-WEALTH — Wealth and mass-affluent

- **POS-WEALTH-1.** Wealth management is a long-running personal strategic theme (HSBC WPB experience explicitly carried into Lloyds). *(Standing position, 2018–present.)*
- **POS-WEALTH-2.** At HSBC, framed the WPB combination as creating "one of the world's largest and fastest growing wealth franchises, centred in Asia". *(HSBC WPB press release, Mar 2020.)*
- **POS-WEALTH-3.** At Lloyds, sees a gap to serve customers with income or wealth above ~£75,000 through an integrated banking + investment proposition. *(2022 strategy refresh.)*
- **POS-WEALTH-4.** Consistent framing: "technology in their pockets, alongside exceptional people who provide advice" — digital tools *plus* human advice, not one or the other. *(Standing position, 2022.)*

## POS-SME — SME and commercial banking

- **POS-SME-1.** Commits to digitising SME banking — target cited that >50% of SME products are offered digitally by 2024. *(2022 strategy refresh, targeted by 2024.)*
- **POS-SME-2.** Targets disciplined growth in corporate and institutional banking around existing strengths (cash management, lending, risk management services). *(2022 strategy refresh.)*

## POS-COSTLIVING — Cost-of-living and customer support

- **POS-COSTLIVING-1.** Publicly committed Lloyds to *proactively contacting* customers who may be facing financial difficulty as inflation rose. *(2022, cost-of-living context.)*
- **POS-COSTLIVING-2.** Frames customer support during macro stress as both right thing to do for society and the right thing to do commercially (it reduces bad-debt risk). *(Standing position, 2022.)*
- **POS-COSTLIVING-3.** Pairs strong financial results with explicit acknowledgement that the macro outlook remains uncertain. *(Standing framing, 2022 results commentary.)*

## POS-RISK — Risk, cyber, and geopolitics

- **POS-RISK-1.** Following Russia's invasion of Ukraine (Feb 2022), publicly stated Lloyds had been on "heightened alert... around our cyber risk controls" for the prior couple of months, with controls tightened and coordination with government on sanctions. *(Feb 2022 commentary.)*
- **POS-RISK-2.** Position on risk: proactive vigilance and explicit governance partnership with authorities, not reactive defensiveness. *(Standing position.)*

## POS-REGULATION — Regulation, policy, and the UK

- **POS-REGULATION-1.** Publicly *welcomed* UK government intervention in the motor finance commission redress matter, in the context of Lloyds having set aside ~£450m for potential redress. *(Davos 2025; provision figure as of 2024 disclosures — not a current-period figure.)*
- **POS-REGULATION-2.** Argues the UK has the chance to "really stand out" globally given its services-led economy, even amid global uncertainty. *(Davos 2025.)*
- **POS-REGULATION-3.** Emphasises the importance of the UK remaining an "investable location" for global investors. *(Standing position, 2024–2025.)*

## POS-CLIMATE — Climate and sustainability

- **POS-CLIMATE-1.** Reaffirmed Lloyds' Net Zero commitments — financed emissions by 2050 (or sooner), own operations by 2030. *(Standing commitments, reaffirmed 2022.)*
- **POS-CLIMATE-2.** Cited green finance targets (~£10bn green mortgages, ~£15bn business green financing by 2024). *(2022 strategy refresh, targeted by 2024.)*
- **POS-CLIMATE-3.** Cited UK green-economy investment requirement of approximately £40–50 billion per year for the next 20 years; positions the private sector — and Lloyds — as needing to help fill the gap. *(As cited 2022.)*
- **POS-CLIMATE-4.** Frames climate as both responsibility and *commercial opportunity*, not one or the other. *(Standing position.)*

## POS-CULTURE — Culture, diversity, and leadership

- **POS-CULTURE-1.** Publicly committed to diversity targets cited as 50% female and 13.5% ethnic-minority representation in senior leadership. *(As cited 2022.)*
- **POS-CULTURE-2.** Argues that reporting on these targets and being authentic in leadership will make Lloyds "a better, fitter organisation... and serve our customers better". *(Standing position, 2022.)*
- **POS-CULTURE-3.** Advises emerging leaders to "get comfortable showing up as your authentic self" rather than playing a role. *(Standing position.)*

## POS-CONTINUITY — Continuity and listening on arrival

- **POS-CONTINUITY-1.** Described joining Lloyds as a "no-brainer" because of its purpose, heritage, and people. *(Aug 2021, Day One.)*
- **POS-CONTINUITY-2.** Described his approach as *"not starting afresh; picking up the baton"* — credits predecessors and emphasises listening before pivoting. *(Aug 2021, Day One.)*
