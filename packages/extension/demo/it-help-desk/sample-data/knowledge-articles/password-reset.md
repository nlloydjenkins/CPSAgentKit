# Password Reset and Account Recovery

## Self-Service Password Reset

If you have forgotten your password or need to change it, you can reset it yourself:

1. Go to [https://passwordreset.microsoftonline.com](https://passwordreset.microsoftonline.com)
2. Enter your Contoso email address
3. Complete the CAPTCHA
4. Verify your identity using one of these methods:
   - Approve a notification in the Microsoft Authenticator app
   - Enter a code sent to your registered phone number
5. Enter a new password that meets the requirements below
6. Confirm the new password
7. Sign in with your new password

## Password Requirements

| Rule           | Requirement                                                                                       |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Minimum length | 12 characters                                                                                     |
| Complexity     | At least one uppercase, one lowercase, one number, and one special character                      |
| History        | Cannot reuse your last 10 passwords                                                               |
| Expiry         | Passwords expire every 90 days — you'll receive email reminders at 14, 7, and 1 day before expiry |
| Lockout        | 5 failed attempts locks the account for 30 minutes                                                |

## Changing Your Password (When You Know the Current One)

1. Press **Ctrl + Alt + Delete** on your keyboard
2. Select **Change a password**
3. Enter your current password, then your new password twice
4. Click **Submit**

Alternatively:

1. Go to [https://myaccount.microsoft.com](https://myaccount.microsoft.com)
2. Click **Password** under Security info
3. Follow the prompts

## Account Locked Out

If your account is locked after too many failed attempts:

- **Wait 30 minutes** and the lockout will clear automatically
- If you cannot wait, contact IT Support through the IT Help Desk agent (using a colleague's Teams account or by calling extension 4400)

## Compromised Account

If you suspect your account has been compromised (unauthorised emails sent, unfamiliar sign-in activity):

1. **Change your password immediately** using the self-service portal
2. **Contact IT Support immediately** — this is treated as a Critical security incident
3. Do NOT delete any suspicious emails — IT may need them for investigation
4. Check your recent sign-in activity at [https://mysignins.microsoft.com](https://mysignins.microsoft.com)

## Important Notes

- **The IT Help Desk agent cannot reset your password.** Password resets must be done through the self-service portal or by contacting IT Support directly. This is a security policy requirement.
- Never share your password with anyone, including IT Support staff. IT will never ask for your password.
- Do not write your password on sticky notes or store it in an unencrypted file.
- Use a password manager (such as the built-in Microsoft Edge password manager) if you have difficulty remembering passwords.

## Contact

For account recovery issues that the self-service portal cannot resolve, contact IT Support through the IT Help Desk agent in Teams.
